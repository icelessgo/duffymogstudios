A **castle wedding in Ireland** is the dream for so many couples — and it's easy to see why. Ireland has more castles per square mile than almost anywhere in Europe, and many of them are now stunning wedding venues. Turrets, stone walls, sweeping estates, and centuries of history — it doesn't get more romantic than that.

As a **wedding photographer** who's shot in castles all over Ireland, here are my top 10 picks for couples looking for that fairytale setting.

## 1. Ashford Castle, Co. Mayo

The crown jewel of **Irish castle wedding venues**. Ashford Castle is an 800-year-old estate on the shores of Lough Corrib, now a five-star hotel. The grounds are magnificent — think manicured gardens, ancient woodlands, a lakeside setting, and interiors dripping with old-world grandeur.

**Best for:** Couples who want the ultimate luxury castle experience with no detail spared.

**Photography highlights:** The lakefront, the stone bridge, the formal gardens, and the oak-panelled interiors.

## 2. Dromoland Castle, Co. Clare

Set on 450 acres of rolling parkland, **Dromoland Castle** has been home to Irish royalty since the 16th century. The grounds include a golf course, a lake, and beautiful walled gardens. Inside, the castle blends historic grandeur with modern elegance.

**Best for:** Larger weddings with guests who love a grand country estate.

**Photography highlights:** The sweeping driveway approach, the lakeside with the castle reflected in the water, and the dramatic main staircase.

## 3. Luttrellstown Castle, Co. Dublin

Just 20 minutes from Dublin city centre, **Luttrellstown Castle** offers 567 acres of private parkland. The Gothic-style castle dates back to the 15th century and is exclusively yours for the day. The estate feels like a world away from the city, with ancient trees, rolling lawns, and a stunningly ornate interior.

**Best for:** Dublin-based couples who want a castle wedding without travelling far.

**Photography highlights:** The Gothic entrance hall, the estate's tree-lined avenue, and the parkland at golden hour.

## 4. Markree Castle, Co. Sligo

One of Ireland's oldest inhabited castles, **Markree Castle** sits on 500 acres in the heart of Yeats Country. The restored interiors are romantic and atmospheric — think stained glass, sweeping staircases, and candlelit dining rooms. The surrounding countryside of Sligo is wild and beautiful.

**Best for:** Couples who want authentic, lived-in castle charm rather than polished hotel luxury.

**Photography highlights:** The grand staircase, the stained-glass windows, and the castle lit up at twilight.

## 5. Cabra Castle, Co. Cavan

A stunning **Georgian-Gothic castle** set in 100 acres of parkland and gardens. **Cabra Castle** has a dramatic stone exterior, beautiful courtyard, and luxurious interiors. The estate also includes a charming gate lodge and stone-walled gardens.

**Best for:** Intimate to mid-sized weddings with a real sense of occasion.

**Photography highlights:** The castle courtyard, the tree-lined entrance, and the atmospheric drawing rooms.

## 6. Kilkea Castle, Co. Kildare

Dating back to 1180, **Kilkea Castle** is one of the oldest inhabited castles in Ireland. Recently restored, it combines medieval character with contemporary luxury. The castle sits in a beautiful rural setting with walled gardens and the River Greese running through the grounds.

**Best for:** Couples who love history and want a castle that feels authentically ancient.

**Photography highlights:** The medieval tower, the walled gardens, and the river.

## 7. Darver Castle, Co. Louth

A hidden gem. **Darver Castle** is a beautifully restored medieval tower house surrounded by manicured gardens and rolling countryside. It's more intimate than the grand estate castles, which gives it a wonderfully personal feel. The gardens are spectacular in every season.

**Best for:** Couples who want an intimate castle wedding with a personal touch.

**Photography highlights:** The castle tower at blue hour, the gardens, and the stone archways.

## 8. Ballyseede Castle, Co. Kerry

Nestled in 30 acres of woodland and gardens in County Kerry, **Ballyseede Castle** is a 15th-century castle with a warm, welcoming atmosphere. Its location makes it a perfect base for exploring the Ring of Kerry and Dingle Peninsula — ideal if you want adventure portraits before or after your wedding.

**Best for:** Destination weddings in the Kerry countryside.

**Photography highlights:** The woodland walks, the castle entrance draped in ivy, and the Kerry mountains as a backdrop.

## 9. Clontarf Castle Hotel, Dublin

A castle wedding without leaving the city. **Clontarf Castle** dates back to 1172 and has been beautifully integrated with a modern hotel. The medieval-themed interiors — think stone walls, suits of armour, and dramatic lighting — create an incredible atmosphere for evening celebrations.

**Best for:** City couples who want castle character with Dublin convenience.

**Photography highlights:** The medieval banquet room, the castle exterior lit at night, and nearby Clontarf promenade for coastal shots.

## 10. Kinnitty Castle, Co. Offaly

At the foot of the Slieve Bloom Mountains, **Kinnitty Castle** is a Gothic Revival castle with a wonderfully dramatic atmosphere. The Great Hall with its roaring fire, stone walls, and high ceilings sets the scene for a truly memorable wedding. The surrounding mountains and forests offer stunning natural backdrops.

**Best for:** Couples who want drama, atmosphere, and a touch of Gothic romance.

**Photography highlights:** The Great Hall fireplace, the castle tower, and the Slieve Bloom Mountains at sunset.

## Tips for Planning a Castle Wedding in Ireland

### Budget Accordingly
Castle venues in Ireland range from approximately **€8,000 to €30,000+** for the venue alone, depending on the size, exclusivity, and time of year. Many include accommodation, catering, and event coordination in their packages.

### Consider the Season
Castles photograph differently in every season. **Summer** gives you long golden evenings and lush gardens. **Autumn** brings warm colours and dramatic skies. **Winter** weddings in castles are incredibly atmospheric — think roaring fires, candlelight, and moody landscapes.

### Think About Guest Accommodation
Many castle venues offer on-site rooms, which is a huge bonus. Guests can arrive the night before, enjoy the setting, and not worry about taxis.

### Plan for Irish Weather
Castles have a massive advantage over other venues: they look incredible rain or shine. Dramatic skies and wet stone actually photograph beautifully. But always have a plan for your ceremony — if you're hoping for an outdoor ceremony in the grounds, make sure there's an indoor backup.

### Book Early
The best castle venues book out **12–24 months in advance**, especially for peak season (May–September). If your heart is set on a particular castle, reach out early.
