Wexford sits in Ireland's sunny southeast corner, and the difference in light quality here is something you notice immediately as a photographer. This county averages more sunshine hours than almost anywhere else in the country, and it shows — the colours are warmer, the skies are wider, and the coastal light has a clarity that gives **wedding photographer Wexford** images a luminous, almost Mediterranean quality on a good day. Between the long sandy beaches of the south coast, the ancient Norman towns, and the gently rolling farmland of the interior, Wexford offers a setting that feels unhurried, warm, and genuinely distinctive.

## Top Wedding Venues in Wexford

**Marlfield House** — A Regency-era country house near Gorey, set in 36 acres of gardens and woodland. The conservatory dining room, with its vine-covered ironwork and glass walls looking into the gardens, is one of the most beautiful reception rooms I have photographed in. The walled garden, the woodland walk, and the ornamental lake give a range of portrait settings within the grounds. The light in the conservatory changes throughout the day and is particularly magical in the late afternoon.

**Wells House & Gardens** — A Victorian Gothic mansion near Ballyedmond, set in a wooded estate with formal terraced gardens, a craft courtyard, and ancient woodland. The house itself is full of period detail — dark wood panelling, ornate fireplaces, a sweeping staircase — while the gardens cascade down a hillside in a series of planted terraces. It is a venue with enormous visual depth.

**Horetown House** — A Georgian country house near Foulksmills in south Wexford, offering exclusive-use weddings on a working farm. The interiors are elegant and understated, and the surrounding farmland — including a walled garden, a tree-lined avenue, and views across the rolling south Wexford countryside — provides the kind of honest, pastoral backdrop that photographs with real authenticity. The informal, homely atmosphere tends to produce some of the most relaxed wedding photography I deliver.

**Clone House** — A restored Georgian house near Aughrim on the Wicklow-Wexford border, with pared-back, whitewashed interiors and a setting among ancient oak trees. The style here is deliberately simple, which suits couples who want the landscape and the people — not the decor — to dominate their photographs.

**Ballinacurra House** — An 18th-century country house near Ballycullane, set on 40 acres of parkland. The house has been restored with a contemporary edge, and the grounds include a walled garden, a croquet lawn, and mature trees that provide shelter and shade for portrait sessions.

## Why Wexford for Your Wedding?

**Wexford wedding photography** benefits from the county's exceptional light. Met Eireann data consistently places Rosslare and the southeast coast among the sunniest spots in Ireland, with average annual sunshine totals around 1,600 hours compared to the national average of roughly 1,400. For photographers, this is significant — more sunshine means more opportunities for golden hour work, more warmth in skin tones, and more variation in the sky.

The coastline is a major draw. The long, curving beaches at Curracloe (where the opening scene of Saving Private Ryan was filmed), Duncannon, and Rosslare Strand are expansive enough to feel private even in summer. The Hook Peninsula, stretching out into Waterford Harbour, offers a sequence of rocky headlands, lighthouses, and ruined Norman churches that provide raw, dramatic portrait locations. The Hook Lighthouse itself — the oldest operational lighthouse in the world — is an extraordinary backdrop.

Inland, Wexford has a character that is distinctly different from the midlands or the west. The farmland is productive and well-tended, the hedgerows are thick and high, and the small market towns — Enniscorthy, New Ross, Bunclody — retain a quiet, unhurried charm. The Blackstairs Mountains along the western border provide a low, rolling horizon that adds depth to landscape portraits without dominating the frame.

## What to Expect from Your Wexford Wedding Photographer

Wexford is approximately 90 minutes to two hours from Dublin depending on the venue location — Gorey in the north is closer to 80 minutes via the M11, while the Hook Peninsula in the south is a full two-hour drive. Travel is included in my standard [pricing packages](/pricing/) for all Wexford venues.

The weather advantage is real and consistent. Wexford receives noticeably less rainfall than Dublin and significantly less than the west coast. Summer afternoons here are often warm and settled when other parts of the country are dealing with showers. That said, exposed coastal locations like Hook Head can be windy, so I always plan sheltered alternatives for portrait sessions.

Golden hour in Wexford is generous. The county's southerly position means longer evenings in summer — the sun does not set until well after 9:30pm in June — and the light takes on a rich, amber quality as it drops towards the western horizon. South-facing coastal venues benefit from light reflecting off the sea, which fills shadows and creates a soft, wraparound glow that is uniquely flattering. For winter weddings, the low sun stays directional and warm-toned throughout the short afternoon, which suits the intimate, candlelit atmosphere of country house venues.
