County Clare occupies a unique place on Ireland's western seaboard — a county where the landscape itself feels ancient and otherworldly. From the lunar limestone pavements of the Burren to the sheer 700-foot drop of the Cliffs of Moher, Clare offers wedding backdrops that are genuinely unlike anywhere else on Earth. As a **wedding photographer in Clare**, I find that the county's raw, elemental beauty elevates every wedding I shoot there. The stone, the sea, and the enormous Atlantic sky combine to produce photographs with a scale and atmosphere that couples treasure for a lifetime.

## Top Wedding Venues in Clare

**Dromoland Castle, Newmarket-on-Fergus** — The ancestral seat of the O'Brien clan, direct descendants of Brian Boru. Dromoland is one of Ireland's finest castle hotels, set on a 450-acre estate with a lake, walled gardens, and centuries-old trees. The interiors are grand without being stuffy, and the grounds provide an extraordinary range of portrait settings — from the formal parterre garden to the wild lakeshore with the castle towering behind. If you're drawn to castle venues, my [guide to Ireland's best castle wedding venues](/blog/best-castle-wedding-venues-ireland/) includes more detail on what Dromoland offers.

**Falls Hotel, Ennistymon** — Overlooking the cascades of the River Inagh in the charming town of Ennistymon, Falls Hotel has a relaxed, unpretentious atmosphere that Clare couples love. The wooded riverside walk is a photographer's dream — dappled light, the sound of rushing water, and a sense of seclusion just steps from the reception. The town itself, with its colourful shop fronts and traditional pubs, adds to the day's character.

**Trump International Golf Links & Hotel, Doonbeg** — Set along a crescent of golden beach with the Atlantic stretching to the horizon, Doonbeg's location is spectacular. The dunes and beach provide a windswept, cinematic setting for portraits, and the interiors are warm and contemporary. Being on the west Clare coast, the sunsets here can be extraordinary.

**The Old Ground Hotel, Ennis** — Right in the centre of Ennis, Clare's vibrant county town, The Old Ground is an 18th-century landmark with a warm, traditional feel. The courtyard garden is a lovely ceremony option, and the proximity to Ennis town centre means guests can enjoy the town's famous trad music scene. It suits couples who want heritage and accessibility in one.

**Gregans Castle Hotel, Ballyvaughan** — A country house hotel at the foot of Corkscrew Hill in the heart of the Burren. Gregans is intimate and quietly luxurious, and its setting is extraordinary — the limestone karst landscape of the Burren stretches in every direction, with Galway Bay visible in the distance. For couples who want something understated and visually striking, it's exceptional.

## Why Clare for Your Wedding?

Clare's defining feature is geological. The Burren — 250 square kilometres of exposed limestone karst — is home to an unlikely mix of Arctic, Alpine, and Mediterranean wildflowers growing side by side in the crevices (grikes) of the rock. In May and June, the Burren is carpeted with gentians, orchids, and bloody cranesbill, and the silver-grey rock glows in the light. Photographically, it's unlike any other landscape in Ireland: clean, sculptural, and endlessly interesting.

Then there's the coast. The Cliffs of Moher are the headline act, and for good reason — the scale is staggering and the views stretch to the Aran Islands and Connemara. But the Clare coastline beyond the cliffs is equally compelling: the sea stacks at Bridges of Ross near Loop Head, the wave-cut platforms at Fanore Beach, the sheltered harbour at Ballyvaughan. Each offers something different for photography.

Clare also has one of the strongest traditional music cultures in Ireland. Towns like Doolin, Ennistymon, and Miltown Malbay host sessions in their pubs most nights of the week, and many Clare weddings naturally incorporate live trad music. These candid, musical moments — a spontaneous reel in the bar, guests linking arms — are the kind of unscripted joy that makes **Clare wedding photography** so rich.

## What to Expect from Your Clare Wedding Photographer

Clare is approximately 2.5 to 3 hours from Dublin, depending on whether your venue is in the east of the county (near Ennis or Shannon) or further out on the coast. I always travel the day before for Clare weddings, which gives me time to check the light at your venue and confirm our portrait plan.

The west Clare coast gets weather — there's no sugarcoating that. But coastal weather is dynamic, and dynamic weather makes for powerful photographs. I've photographed couples on Fanore Beach with horizontal rain one minute and golden sunshine the next, and those are images they come back to again and again. I carry full weather-sealed gear and always have sheltered backup locations mapped.

One practical note: if you're hosting your ceremony in one of Clare's beautiful old churches — many of which have thick stone walls and relatively small windows — I bring portable lighting to ensure we capture the ceremony beautifully regardless of the natural light available.
