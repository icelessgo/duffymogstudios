County Mayo occupies a singular place in the Irish wedding landscape. It is home to Ashford Castle — arguably the most famous wedding venue in the country — but it is also a county of wild blanket bog, soaring sea cliffs on Achill Island, and the quietly beautiful harbour town of Westport. As a **wedding photographer Mayo** couples choose again and again, I find that Mayo weddings carry a sense of occasion that is hard to replicate anywhere else. The grandeur of the venues meets the rugged honesty of the west coast, and the photographs tell that story naturally.

## Top Wedding Venues in Mayo

**Ashford Castle** — An 800-year-old castle on the shores of Lough Corrib, Ashford Castle needs little introduction. The grounds are immaculately kept, the interiors are lavish without being gaudy, and the lakeside setting provides endless portrait opportunities. Photographing here means working with stone corridors, candlelit hallways, and sweeping lawns — every corner offers something different.

**Westport House** — One of Ireland's great Georgian estates, Westport House sits above Clew Bay with views toward Croagh Patrick. The house itself has ornate plasterwork ceilings and a dramatic staircase, while the surrounding parkland provides softer, more natural settings for couple portraits.

**The Ice House Hotel** — Perched on the banks of the River Moy in Ballina, the Ice House is a boutique property built into a restored Victorian ice store. Its contemporary architecture and riverside terrace make it a favourite for couples who want something modern against a natural backdrop. The reflections on the Moy at dusk are particularly photogenic.

**Mulranny Park Hotel** — Sitting between Clew Bay and the Nephin Beg mountains, Mulranny offers an Atlantic panorama that stretches to Clare Island. The old railway gardens are a hidden gem for portraits, and the wild beach at Mulranny is just minutes away for more adventurous shoots.

**Knockranny House Hotel** — Overlooking Westport town with Croagh Patrick framed in the distance, Knockranny is elegant and intimate. The views from the terrace at sunset are extraordinary, with the reek's conical silhouette glowing against the western sky.

## Why Mayo for Your Wedding?

Mayo's defining feature is Croagh Patrick. Ireland's holy mountain dominates the skyline from Westport to Louisburgh, and its distinctive cone appears in wedding photographs as a natural landmark that instantly places the image. Clew Bay, scattered with its 365 drumlin islands, adds a coastal element that is unique in Ireland — the bay's sheltered waters reflect light differently from the open Atlantic, producing softer blues and greens.

Achill Island, connected to the mainland by bridge, offers another dimension entirely: towering cliffs at Croaghaun, the deserted village at Slievemore, and vast empty beaches at Keem Bay. Couples willing to build in time for an Achill portrait session are always rewarded with images that feel almost otherworldly.

The town of Westport itself adds practical charm. It is compact, walkable, and full of character — colourful shopfronts, the tree-lined Mall along the Carrowbeg River, and a pub culture that guarantees lively post-wedding sessions. Guests love it because there is plenty to do beyond the wedding day.

Mayo is approximately three and a half hours from Dublin, and I typically travel the day before to allow time for a venue walkthrough and light check. **Mayo wedding photography** demands flexibility with weather. The west coast is wetter than the east, but that moisture is what makes the landscape so intensely green and atmospheric. I always have a Plan B for portraits — Ashford Castle's interior galleries, Westport House's grand rooms, or even the covered terrace at the Ice House — but I will always encourage stepping outside if conditions allow, because the drama of Mayo's skies adds enormous character to images.

Golden hour in summer is late and long. A June wedding in Westport will see the sun dipping behind Clare Island around 10 pm, painting the sky in shades of coral and violet. I plan the day's timeline to make the most of this.
