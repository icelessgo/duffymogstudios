↳ where it all started

I fell in love with cameras before I knew what an f-stop was.

It started with a battered secondhand film camera and a complete disregard for the cost of developing photos I wasn't sure would even turn out. I was hooked before I had any idea what I was doing.

Over the years that obsession took me through fashion shoots, commercial work, events, and portraits — building up a toolkit and a way of seeing the world that I didn't fully appreciate until I shot my first wedding and realised: this is it. This is the thing.

Weddings have everything — tension, joy, chaos, tenderness, terrible dancing, and the most overwhelming love you'll ever be in the room with. I was done for from the first one.

✦
[ Photo of Gabriela — behind the scenes, candid ]
↳ what changed everything

Then I became a parent. And weddings suddenly meant something different.

When you have a child, you start to understand what it means to look back. You understand the weight of a photograph. The way a single image can hold an entire feeling — a whole day — a whole version of yourself that you'll only ever visit again through pictures.

That shifted how I approach every wedding I shoot. I'm not just capturing a pretty day. I'm making the photos your family will look at for the next fifty years. The ones that end up framed, dog-eared, passed around at Christmas. That's not something I take lightly — and it's exactly why I care so much about getting it right.

Based in Dublin. Travelling all of Ireland. Absolutely no awkward posing.

How I Actually Work

no shot lists, no stiff poses, no nonsense ✦
🎞️

Documentary First

I follow your day, not a checklist. The goal is to capture what actually happens — the nerves, the relief, the chaos, the quiet moments in between — not to manufacture something that looks nice on Instagram.

🤝

I Get to Know You First

Every couple gets a pre-wedding consultation so we actually know each other before the big day. Camera-shy? Tell me. Hate being centre of attention? Also fine. I'll work with you, not at you.

🔍

The Details Matter

The dress tag with a note from your mum. Your gran doing something unhinged on the dancefloor. Your partner's face in the two seconds before they see you. I live for those shots. I will find them.
